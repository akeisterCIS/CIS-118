Background Information

Gawr Gura (がうる・ぐら) is a female English-speaking Virtual YouTuber associated with hololive, debuting in 2020 as part of hololive English first generation "-Myth-" alongside Ninomae Ina'nis, Takanashi Kiara, Watson Amelia and Mori Calliope.

Gura is currently the most subscribed VTuber worldwide, taking the #1 spot from industry pioneer Kizuna AI in 2021. As of November 2022, Gura has over 4 million YouTube subscribers.

Gura is friendly and readily likeable, and often amuses her viewers with foolish antics. She has no sense of direction, often misspells and mispronounces words, has trouble remembering her own age, and consistently fails to solve basic math problems, leading viewers to affectionately call her a "dum shark". Memorably, one viewer declared that "Gura has a heart of gold and a head of bone."

However, despite her poor math and spelling skills, Gura is quick-witted, clever, and has vast knowledge of Shark Facts. When questioned on why she was not "boing boing," she excused it by claiming that she was "hydrodynamic." Her skill with fast-paced rhythm games is exceptional, and she is highly ambitious. She also occasionally makes lewd jokes, and is familiar with modern meme culture, which she references often.

Timeline of Milestones
2020 Debuted in September. She was 12 minutes late to her debut stream due to a technical issue. In order to prepare for the stream, she watched many Hololive JP streamers and their debut streams to prepare. Just 13 days after her debut, she reached 500,000 subscribers on Youtube. 37 days after her debut, she overtook Shirakami Fubuki and Inugami Korone as the most subscribed Hololive Vtuber on Youtube.
2021 Reached 3 million subscribers on Youtube, becoming the first Hololive meber to do so as well as the first Vtuber in general.
2022 Reached 4 million subscribers on Youtube. Debuted her original animation "Shark'd" which depicts Gura's arrival on land and how she got her clothes and exposure to Hololive. The animation was produced by San Francisco based studio Tonari Animation, who also released a "making of" video.
